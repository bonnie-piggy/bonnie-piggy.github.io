“What is Fluxus?”

it calls for:

"Purge the world of 'EUROPANISM'!"
"Destroy the boundary between art and life."


What:

Fluxus encourages chance-based process over the finished product.

This is an experimental art movement that blends performance, chance, everyday objects, and humor to challenge traditional art.

Who:

George Maciunas,
Yoko Ono, Nam June Paik and many international collaborators

They are a diverse community of artists who influenced each other, and also, largely, friends.

When:

1960s – 1970s

Fluxus emerged in the early 1960s and continued evolving through the 1970s and beyond.

Where:

The Globe

It began in the US and Europe, especially in New York, but quickly spread worldwide.

Maciunas, Fluxus Manifesto, Düsseldorf, 1963.

George Maciunas

George Maciunas on a 2016 postage stamp of Lithuania

He is an artist, art historian, and art organizer.

In 1963, Maciunas composed the first Fluxus Manifesto, (see above).
He established the official Fluxus Headquarters in New York City and proceeded to make Fluxus into a sort of multinational corporation.

Perhaps most important of all of Maciunas's publishing activities remain the object multiples, conceived as inexpensive, mass-produced unlimited editions. These were either works made by individual Fluxus artists, sometimes in collaboration with Maciunas, or, most controversially, Maciunas's own interpretations of an artist's concept or score. Their purpose was to erode the cultural status of art and to help to eliminate the artist's ego.

Yoko Uno

Yoko Ono. This Is Not Here. 1971.

She is a retired Japanese artist, musician, and activist.

She became widely known outside the fine art world in 1969
when she married English musician John Lennon of the Beatles.

As Lennon's widow, Ono works to preserve his legacy. She funded the Strawberry Fields memorial in Manhattan's Central Park, the Imagine Peace Tower in Iceland, and the John Lennon Museum in Saitama, Japan.
